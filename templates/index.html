Abstract

Corn is the main food crop after rice in Indonesia. So it has great potential in agriculture. As in general plants, this plant also has a variety of diseases and can interfere with its quality. Corn plants are highly susceptible to common foliar diseases such rust, gray spot, and northern blight. Symptoms of disease in corn plants can be seen from changes in the morphology of corn leaves. Classical machine learning methods, where manual feature extraction must be perfect to achieve optimal results, the need for models that can classify well. In this study, the EfficientNetB5 deep learning model was proposed to classify foliar diseases of maize plants and model performance was compared between hyperparameter learning rate and batch size. All models were trained with public datasets obtained from Mendeley data with a total of 3,852 images divided into 4 classes, namely, 1,162 healthy, 1,192 common rust, 985 northern leaf blight, and 513 cercospora leaf spots. Then, the process model validation uses 5-cross validation to get the best model results. The results obtained in testing the dataset show that the combination of learning rate 0.0001 and batch size 32 achieves the highest value compared to other models. The evaluation values obtained were 96.27% accuracy, 90.90% precision, 97.55% specificity, and 88.13% sensitivity.
